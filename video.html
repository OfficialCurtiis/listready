iPhone video → MP4 questions

Why won’t my iPhone videos play on Windows?

iPhones record in HEVC (H.265), Apple’s high-efficiency video codec. Windows doesn’t play HEVC out of the box — you’d normally need a paid codec from the Microsoft Store. Converting the video to standard MP4 (H.264) fixes it: it plays in any Windows app with no extra software.

Is my video uploaded to a server?

No. The conversion runs entirely in your browser, on your own machine, using a software video engine. Your video never leaves your computer.

How long does it take?

It depends on the clip. Because it runs in software on your device (no special hardware), a short clip takes a minute or two; longer or 4K videos take longer and use a lot of memory. Short clips work best.

Is the video converter free?

Short clips — under about 1 minute and 80 MB — convert free. Longer or larger videos need a one-time $9 Pro unlock, which also covers unlimited photo batches and watermarks on the main tool. (Converting video is heavy work that runs on your own device, so Pro keeps it sustainable.)

What formats can I convert?

iPhone .MOV and .MP4 videos (HEVC or H.264) → standard H.264 MP4. The result plays on Windows, Android, and just about anything.
